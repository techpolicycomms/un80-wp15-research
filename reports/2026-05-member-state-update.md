## Why this matters

The UN system spends more than **$2 billion annually** on ICT. A baseline across **28 entities** (covering **97%** of expenditure) confirms significant **overlap in core services** and **interoperability challenges** — patterns consistent with literature on cloud economies of scale and shared-service delivery in large organizations [^1][^2]. Work Package 15 addresses this through **Action 61** (ICT consolidation) and **Action 62** (Technology Accelerator Platform), with public secondary evidence now monitored on a recurring automated basis.

## Our goal

Reduce duplication, improve interoperability, and accelerate responsible digital innovation [^3] — while keeping a clear **human review gate** on all outputs derived from this research hub.

## Action 61 — ICT consolidation

### Current phase

**Consolidation proposal — in progress.** Baseline complete; implementation plan pending CEB / Steering Committee direction.

### Progress since last update

- Public evidence reinforces that **UNICC** is not only a consolidation anchor but an **operational shared backbone**: UNIQCloud private cloud (2025), cybersecurity exercises, and ongoing pooled platform procurement.
- New overlap signal: mature shared cloud coexists with entity-level spend — consolidation opportunity is **scaling standardization**, not starting from zero [^1][^5].

## Action 62 — Technology Accelerator Platform

### Portfolio status

**Endorsed portfolio complete** (digital ID, expertise on demand, modern conferencing). **Long-term TAP concept — in progress.**

### Innovation pipeline highlights

- **ODET** (UN Office for Digital and Emerging Technologies, est. Jan 2025) is the system-wide digital focal point — TAP should align with its AI, DPI, and open-source coordination streams.
- **Digital Cooperation Portal** supports initiative mapping under the Global Digital Compact — relevant to TAP demand sensing.
- **UN Global Pulse** and **UN Innovation Network** updates show active cross-entity innovation activity compatible with TAP's accelerator role.
- **W3C Verifiable Credentials** and **Digital Public Goods Alliance** (prior scan) support digital ID and reusable solution design [^4][^6].
