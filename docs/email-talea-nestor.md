## 1. To your boss (send this first)

**To:** [Boss name]  
**Subject:** Request to pilot automated WP15 research support — permission to approach Talea & Nestor

Dear [Boss name],

I would like your guidance on a small pilot idea to support **UN80 Work Package 15**, focused on **Action 61** (ICT consolidation) and **Action 62** (Technology Accelerator Platform).

**The idea**

Set up a lightweight **research and monitoring hub** that would help our team (and WP15 leads) stay on top of public secondary evidence — landscape changes, relevant standards, shared-services developments, and TAP-related signals — without starting from scratch each week.

The hub would combine:

1. **Structured research data** — baseline metrics, source registry, and action tracker in a versioned repository  
2. **A simple dashboard** — Action 61/62 status and key indicators at a glance  
3. **Scheduled AI-assisted monitoring** — agents scan public sources on a fixed cadence, draft updates, and open items for human review (nothing treated as final without sign-off)  
4. **Optional social monitoring** — public posts from major UN agencies on X, Facebook, and YouTube, filtered for WP15-relevant topics  
5. **A Notion workspace** — briefings, landscape scans, and a sources inbox for editorial review  

All outputs would remain **DRAFT working documents**, based on **public secondary data only**, and would not represent an agreed UN position.

**Why I think it is worth exploring**

- **Action 61** needs ongoing public evidence on consolidation, interoperability, and shared services (e.g. ICC)  
- **Action 62** needs a steady pipeline of TAP-relevant sources (digital ID, expertise platforms, conferencing)  
- **Efficiency** — automate the repetitive scanning and drafting; humans focus on judgment and approval  
- **Traceability** — versioned history and a clear review gate before anything is shared externally  

**What I am asking for**

1. Your **green light** to develop and run this as a **small internal pilot** tied to WP15  
2. Permission to **approach Talea and Nestor** to see whether the concept would be useful to their work and to agree scope, governance, and review expectations before anything goes wider  

I have **not** shared this with Talea, Nestor, or anyone outside our team. If you approve, I would propose a short introductory conversation with them — 20 minutes — to walk through the concept and ask what would actually help, rather than presenting something as already decided.

**Governance I would propose**

- Public secondary sources only in any shared repository  
- Internal surveys and sensitive material kept out unless explicitly agreed  
- Human review before external distribution  
- Clear labelling that all outputs are draft working documents  

Happy to discuss timing, resourcing, and whether this fits current priorities. I can also prepare a one-page outline or a brief demo if that would help your decision.

Best regards,  
Rahul
