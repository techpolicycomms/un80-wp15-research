## Why this matters

The UN system baseline indicates more than **$2 billion annually** in ICT spend across **28 entities**, covering **97%** of the expenditure assessed. The consolidation case is not simply cost reduction: cloud and shared-service literature points to resource pooling, measured service, standardization, and common infrastructure as mechanisms that can reduce duplication while improving resilience and interoperability [^1][^2]. For Member States, the relevant question is whether WP15 can convert an already visible shared-service landscape into a clearer system-wide operating model.

## Our goal

WP15 aims to reduce duplication, improve interoperability, and accelerate responsible operational modernization [^3]. Action 61 addresses the ICT backbone - cloud, networks, office software, systems, cybersecurity, and related shared platforms. Action 62 addresses the innovation mechanism - a Technology Accelerator Platform that can identify demand, shape a portfolio, and support implementation without creating parallel digital governance structures.
