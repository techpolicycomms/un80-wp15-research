## Why this matters

The UN system baseline indicates more than **$2 billion annually** in ICT spend across **28 entities**, covering **97%** of the expenditure assessed. June public monitoring adds a policy-relevant question for Member States: how far shared ICT, data, identity, and innovation mechanisms can become the normal operating model when they improve value, resilience, and interoperability. Cloud and shared-service literature supports the logic that pooled infrastructure, standard service models, and measured shared services can reduce avoidable duplication when paired with governance and adoption mechanisms [^1][^2]. Public digital-transformation research also cautions that technology modernization requires institutional capability, ownership, and cross-organizational change, not only common tools [^3].

## Our goal

WP15 aims to reduce duplication, improve interoperability, and accelerate responsible operational modernization. Action 61 focuses on the ICT backbone: cloud, networks, office software, systems, cybersecurity, shared platforms, and related procurement. Action 62 focuses on the innovation mechanism: a Technology Accelerator Platform that can identify common demand, shape a reusable portfolio, mobilize pooled capacity, and support delivery without duplicating existing UN digital coordination structures.

## Action 62 - Technology Accelerator Platform

### Portfolio status

**Initial endorsed portfolio - complete.** The repository continues to track three endorsed use cases: digital ID solutions, digital platforms for access to expertise on demand, and expanded toolkits for modern conferencing.

**Long-term TAP concept - in progress.** June public evidence reinforces TAP as a portfolio and execution mechanism for shared digital solutions, not as a replacement for ODET, DTN, UN Innovation Network, UNICC, or entity-level innovation teams.

### Innovation pipeline highlights

- Public UN80 progress materials describe TAP phase-one solution areas that align with the repo portfolio: AI-assisted conferencing and translation, expertise-on-demand, and common digital identity.
- The public [CEB UN Digital ID](https://unsceb.org/topics/un-digital-id) page describes a system-wide workforce identity intended to reduce data duplication and fragmentation, support secure exchange of personal and professional data, and enable use cases such as UNJSPF pension processing, BSAFE security training, and inter-agency staff movements.
- [UNICC's public AI Hub](https://www.unicc.org/artificial-intelligence/) and [UN Innovation Network digital-community material](https://www.uninnovation.network/un-group-pages/digital) provide reusable operational analogues for TAP: shared AI expertise, secure environments, reusable playbooks, testing spaces, practitioner discovery, and solution showcases.
- Standards-body sources added in June - W3C Digital Credentials, W3C Verifiable Credentials work, DIDs, OASIS Lightweight Verifiable Credential Schema, and ISO identity-management reference material - reinforce that TAP digital ID work should prioritize interoperability, user consent, privacy, and multi-vendor portability [^4].
- Official OICT/ODET social monitoring adds a live signal around UN Open Source Week 2026, open-source programme offices, digital public infrastructure, AI, digital cooperation, and reusable tools.

## Expected improvements

- Reduced duplication in ICT services through clearer shared-service, shared-procurement, and shared-platform options.
- Improved interoperability across the UN system by pairing common infrastructure with identity, data, software, and open-standards governance.
- Accelerated operational modernization through a TAP mechanism that can surface demand, select reusable use cases, mobilize pooled capacity, and transition successful solutions to accountable owners.

## Next steps

1. Advance the Action 61 consolidation proposal with a migration view across cloud, network, office software, systems, cybersecurity, shared software, and procurement channels.
2. Use the June public evidence to sharpen the duplication test: when a shared approach appears better placed to deliver value, entity-specific duplication should require a clear justification and risk rationale.
3. Keep TAP aligned with ODET, DTN, UN Digital ID, UNICC, and UN Innovation Network so that Action 62 strengthens existing system mechanisms rather than adding a parallel coordination layer.
4. Treat social-monitor false positives as monitor-quality feedback; continue using only verified official/public sources in Member State language.
